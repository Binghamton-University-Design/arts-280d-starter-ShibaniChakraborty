Use References

Some people may think that using references is cheating, when really that's not the case. It's how you learn to learn to draw things like body anatomy and nature. You're not born knowing how to draw everything after all.

24 JUNE, 2020

Keep a Sketchbook

Keeping a sketchbook is really useful when you want to practice drawing certain things and you want to keep the art in one place. Also, it's important to look back at old sketchbooks to see how much you've improved (no matter how embarrassing it may be).

24 JUNE, 2020

Limit Your Color Palette

Limiting your palette makes the piece feel more cohesive as a whole. But if you do want to use more colors, make sure they work together. Change the values and hues of the colors so that they work together.

16 AUG, 2020

Sketch in Ballpoint Pen

For a long time, I didn't know that artists did this, but sketching in a ballpoint pen has many benefits. Because you can't erase, doing this activity will help you be more mindful with your strokes, and so it'll help you learn faster.

16 AUG, 2020

Expensive Art Supplies Don't Make Your Art Better

The skills that you develop are more important than what you draw with. There are people out there that make amazing art with cheap supplies or a limited amount of them, so focus on developing your skills as well as figuring out which supplies you want to use long term.

24 JUNE, 2020

Have Fun

Just have fun with your artwork and experiment. You'll become a great artist eventually!
